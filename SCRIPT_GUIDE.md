# SCRIPT_GUIDE (Optional Helper)

This file is **optional** and is only created to help with planning and recording the project video.
It is **not required** for the notebook grade and does not change project requirements.

## Purpose
- Keep the video aligned with `03-FinalProject.ipynb`.
- Keep explanations non-technical and easy to follow.
- Keep timing within the 3-5 minute requirement.

## High-Level Talking Flow (Suggested)
- **Opening (20-30s):** Project question and why it matters in everyday terms.
- **Method (45-60s):** Explain passive vs active use, and that Dataset #1 is the main dataset while others are supporting checks.
- **Main Findings (60-90s):** Passive-style use is more consistently associated with worse wellbeing proxies; active posting is weaker/mixed.
- **Limitations (30-45s):** Observational data, self-report bias, proxy-variable limits, and no causal claims.
- **Takeaway (20-30s):** Clear correlational conclusion and one practical next step.

## Role Mapping (Suggested)
- **Person 3 (script lead):** Finalize wording, sequence, and timing.
- **Person 4 (front half presenter):** Deliver question, motivation, and simple method.
- **Person 5 (closing presenter):** Deliver findings, limitations, final takeaway, and ensure video link is added to notebook.

## Messaging Rules
- Use plain language; avoid technical jargon where possible.
- Do not make causal claims ("causes", "leads to"); use correlational wording ("is associated with").
- Keep statements consistent with notebook Discussion and Conclusion.
- Mention uncertainty and limitations clearly.

## Timing Guardrails
- Aim for ~4 minutes total to stay safe within the 3-5 minute window.
- Suggested split: 1.5 min intro/method, 1.5 min findings, 1 min limitations/takeaway.

## Final Pre-Upload Checklist
- Video claims match `03-FinalProject.ipynb`.
- No causal language.
- Audio is clear and pacing is steady.
- Public link works and is placed in the notebook video section.
